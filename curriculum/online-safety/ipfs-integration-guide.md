# Student Guide: Decentralized Artifact Preservation (IPFS)

**Phase:** 4 / Module 6
**Lead:** @Academy-Instructor

## 1. Introduction to IPFS
IPFS (InterPlanetary File System) is a peer-to-peer network for storing and sharing data in a distributed file system. Unlike traditional servers, IPFS uses **Content Addressing** to identify files by their unique cryptographic hash.

## 2. Why Sovereignty Matters
In the DAA ecosystem, we believe students should own their digital artifacts. By uploading your work to IPFS:
- Your project remains accessible even if central servers go offline.
- You have a permanent, immutable record of your achievement.
- You maintain full control over your digital legacy.

## 3. Technical Workflow
1. **Hash Generation:** Your artifact is processed to generate a unique 'Qm...' hash.
2. **Network Broadcast:** The artifact is pinned to the local DAA IPFS cluster.
3. **Portal Ingestion:** Your IPFS hash is added to the Global Command Center manifest for showcase.

## 4. Security Note
Always ensure your artifacts are sanitized of PII before hashing. IPFS is a permanent public record.
