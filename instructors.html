📚 Curriculum Overview

Our 12-week curriculum is designed to be modular and hands-on, taking students from foundational GPIO control to fully autonomous robotics.

Phase 1: GPIO & Hardware Integration

Focuses on foundational Python programming and microcontroller interfacing. Students learn to control physical hardware using event-driven logic.

View Phase 1 Guide

Phase 2: Parametric CAD & Digital Fabrication

Introduces algorithmic geometry generation and rapid prototyping. Students design parts in code and fabricate them using laser cutters and 3D printers.

View Phase 2 Guide

Phase 3: Autonomous Systems & Sensor Fusion

The capstone phase where students program mobile rovers to navigate autonomously using sensor data and advanced algorithms.

View Phase 3 Guide

Phase 4: Online Safety & Apple Ecosystem

A specialized curriculum focused on responsible digital citizenship, privacy sovereignty, and secure creation within the Apple ecosystem.

View Online Safety Guide

❓ Instructor FAQ

Do I need to be a robotics expert?

While technical background helps, our "Instructor Guides" are designed to walk you through each phase. We also provide "Train the Trainer" sessions.

What is the "Design-by-Code" methodology?

It's our proprietary approach to CAD where students use Python to generate geometry. This bridges the gap between software engineering and mechanical design.

Can I use DAA curriculum in my school?

Yes! We offer licensing for educational partners. Check our Partners page for details.
